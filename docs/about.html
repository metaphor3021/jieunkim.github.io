Jieun Kim is a designer and maker who works primarily with discarded paper,

creating sculptural objects that blur the boundaries between design and art.

Drawn to the unpredictability of handcraft, she explores how fragile,

everyday materials can be reimagined into new forms of utility and perception.

Her work transforms paper that has already served its purpose into objects

that gently disrupt visual and tactile expectations,

encouraging a renewed awareness of material and touch.


Born in Korea and based in the Netherlands, she is currently studying at Design Academy Eindhoven,

where she continues to explore the boundaries between material transformation and contemporary craft.
